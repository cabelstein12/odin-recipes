Deep Dish Pizza

Facts about this dish:

From Wikipedia: According to Tim Samuelson, Chicago's official cultural historian, there is not enough documentation to determine with certainty who invented Chicago-style deep-dish pizza. It is often reported that Chicago-style deep-dish pizza was invented at Pizzeria Uno in Chicago, in 1943, by Uno's founder Ike Sewell. However, a 1956 article from the Chicago Daily News asserts that Uno's original pizza chef Rudy Malnati developed the recipe, and Michele Mohr from the Chicago Tribune reports that the menu at Rosati's Authentic Chicago Pizza has included deep-dish since it opened in 1926, according to the descendants of Saverio Rosati.

Cooking Instructions

  1. grab oatmeal package from pantry
  2. tear package open
  3. pour contents into favorite bowl
  4. fill kettle and boil water
  5. once water boils, pour water into bowl
  6. accidentally scald yourself on piping hot boiling water
  7. run burnt hand under cool water for 10 minutes
  8. after this time your oatmeal gets cold
  9. assess the situation, either:
    • microwave oatmeal
    • throw away oatmeal and repeat from step 1
  10. garnish with chosen toppings
  11. ENJOY
